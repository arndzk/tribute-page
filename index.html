Marcus Aurelius

Chronology

  • AD 121 26 April: Marcus is born in Rome
  • c. AD 121 Death of Marcus' natural father, M. Annius Verus. Adopted by his grandfather.
  • AD 138 Adopted (together with Lucius Ceionius Commodus) by his uncle Antoninus, who became emperor in succession to Hadrian on 10 July.
    Betrothed to Antoninus' daughter Faustina.
  • AD 140 Consul for the first time.
  • AD 145 Married to Faustina.
  • AD 147 Birth of first child.
  • c. AD 155 - 161 Death of Marcus' mother, Domitia Lucilla.
  • AD 161 7 March: Death of Antoninus Pius. Succession of Marcus and Lucius as joint emperors.
    31 August: Birth of son Commodus and twin brother.
  • AD 166-7 Plague in Rome and throughout the empire.
  • AD 168-80 Most of these years spend on campaign on the northern frontiers (in central and south-eastern Europe north of the Danube.
  • AD 169 Death of Lucius.
  • AD 175 Rebellion of Avidius Cassius, governor of Syria, in the east.
    Death of Faustina at Halala in Cappadocia.
  • AD 177 Commodus (aged 15), Marcus' only surviving son, made joint emperor.
  • AD 180 17 March: Died near Sirmium in Pannonia.
    Succession of Commodus as sole emperor.
